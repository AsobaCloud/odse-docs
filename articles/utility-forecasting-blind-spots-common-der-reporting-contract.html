Utility Forecasting Blind Spots: Why DER Reporting Needs a Common Contract

Forecast quality is constrained by input consistency long before model quality becomes the bottleneck.

Target audience: Utilities / Grid Planning

Utilities cannot forecast what they cannot consistently measure. Mixed intervals, inconsistent error semantics, and incompatible timestamp treatment introduce forecast bias and planning noise.

Why This Matters

When DER inputs are unstable, dispatch strategies over-hedge with expensive supply buffers and under-utilize distributed generation potential.

Contract-First Reporting

A common reporting contract should define timestamp precision, direction semantics, error taxonomy, and minimum validation requirements.

Practical Pattern

Require submissions in ODSE-compatible shape or enforce transform-at-ingestion for declared OEM sources.

Outcome

Forecast pipelines improve because variance from data-format mismatch is removed before model inference.
